Bolivia — Fuel Subsidy Crisis & Civil Unrest Monitoring
As of 17 July 2026
Key Insights
Current Situation
  • The diesel/GLP shortage has deepened this week, not eased: as of 14 July, 60% of foreign-trade trucks remain paralysed for lack of diesel; black-market diesel now sells at Bs 15–18/litre (up from Bs 11.50–14 last week) vs. the official Bs 9.80. GLP (cooking gas) shortages have also emerged. The transport sector has called a national assembly for 29 July.
  • Evo Morales issued a direct threat from Chapare on 13 July: "the people will break the state of exception, from Santa Cruz." Cochabamba's mayor responded by demanding the government retake control of Chapare within two weeks.
  • Correction: the judiciary-budget standoff was actually defused on 1 July via a 40–50% budget increase pledge, not left open toward a later deadline as previously stated here. Full implementation of the judiciary's core demand remains under negotiation as of 11 July.
  • No new road blockades this week; the Túpac Katari federation's conciliatory leadership (since 9 Jul) appears to be holding.
Key Implications
  • The fuel/GLP crisis is now the dominant operational risk — it's worsening, has a concrete new flashpoint (29 Jul assembly), and directly affects logistics in Santa Cruz.
  • Morales's direct threat this week raises Chapare/Cochabamba risk specifically; no sign yet of it translating into new blockades elsewhere.

Shortage of Essential Supplies
 Elevated ↘ Deteriorating The diesel/GLP shortage has deepened this week rather than easing: as of 14 July, 60% of foreign-trade trucks remain paralysed for lack of diesel [economy.com.bo, 14 Jul]; black-market diesel now sells at Bs 15–18/litre (up from Bs 11.50–14 last week) vs. the official Bs 9.80 [El Diario, 14 Jul]. A GLP (cooking gas) shortage has also emerged, driven by winter demand and a distributor's cylinder shortfall. YPFB's earlier "end of month" normalisation promise looks increasingly unlikely to hold. The transport sector has called a national assembly for 29 July.
👁 What to watch▲
29 July transport assembly. A national "ampliado" of the transport sector is scheduled for 29 July over unresolved fuel shortages — a concrete date where escalation (strikes, blockades) becomes more likely if conditions haven't improved.
