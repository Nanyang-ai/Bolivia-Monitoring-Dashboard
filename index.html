Bolivia — Fuel Subsidy Crisis & Civil Unrest Monitoring
As of 10 July 2026
Key Insights
Current Situation
  • Túpac Katari leader Vicente Salazar was arrested (4 Jul), charged with terrorism (5 Jul), and jailed for 6 months (6 Jul). His federation replaced him with a conciliatory new leader on 9 Jul, easing tension.
  • A real diesel/gas shortage — unrelated to the earlier blockades — is hitting Santa Cruz and Beni this week, with black-market fuel well above official prices. Drivers have given the government an ultimatum; YPFB says normal supply returns by end of month.
  • No new road blockades this week despite the volatility above.
Key Implications
  • Salazar's arrest tested re-mobilisation risk and it held — no blockades resulted, and the leadership change eased tension further.
  • The fuel shortage is a new, separate risk driver worth watching independently of the political conflict, especially in Santa Cruz.

Acts of Violence / Excessive Use of Force / Potential Coup d'État
 Elevated → No Change No street violence reported this week. Rated Elevated given the arrest and imprisonment of a major protest leader (Salazar) on terrorism charges, plus a separate Santa Cruz Civic Committee complaint alleging "armed uprising" against Salazar, Morales, and Argollo [Última Hora, 6 Jul] — both signal an intensifying prosecutorial track even without street incidents. The week's volatility was partially offset by the 9 July leadership change toward a more conciliatory posture.
👁 What to watch▲
Morales case trajectory. Morales remains a fugitive under a standing arrest warrant; the government continues pursuing him as an alleged instigator of the blockades. His Chapare supporters have previously warned of "insurgency" if he's arrested — that risk is unchanged this week.
State of Emergency Declared / Curfews
 Elevated → No Change The state of exception (Supreme Decree 5636, declared 20 June) remained in force throughout this week with no change in status; it runs until 20 September 2026 if not lifted or renewed earlier [Infobae, 9 Jul]. No nationwide curfew is in effect. Notably, the driver confederation stated this week it may protest "despite" the state of exception if fuel demands aren't met — an early test of the decree's practical limits.
Shortage of Essential Supplies
 Elevated ↘ Deteriorating Correction from last week's assessment: a genuine diesel and GLP shortage — independent of the earlier blockades — is affecting the country this week, most acutely in Santa Cruz department towns (San Ignacio de Velasco, Guarayos, Montero) and Beni (Trinidad), where black-market diesel sells at Bs 11.50–14/litre versus the official Bs 9.80 [La Razón, 9 Jul]. YPFB attributes delays to new fuel-quality control protocols and says normalisation won't come until end of July; Confeagro rejects that timeline, calling productive losses already irreversible [Agenda Minera, 9 Jul].
👁 What to watch▲
Driver confederation ultimatum. A 48-hour ultimatum issued 7 July for a government meeting had not been confirmed as resolved as of 9 July reporting; the union has threatened "convulsión" if unmet, despite the state of exception.
